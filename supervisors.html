Lead Academic Supervisor

Research leadership, mentorship, and scholarly contributions.

Dr. Ahmed Sherif, Senior Member, IEEE

Associate Professor, School of Computing Sciences & Computer Engineering,
University of Southern Mississippi (USM)

Dr. Ahmed Sherif is the driving force behind SPEN Lab's research initiatives. As founder and director of the Security and Privacy of Emerging Networks (SPEN) Research Lab at USM, he leads a diverse team of graduate and undergraduate students in pioneering research across several cutting-edge fields.

Academic and Professional Background

  • Ph.D. in Electrical and Computer Engineering - Tennessee Technological University (2017)
  • M.S. - Egypt Japan University of Science & Technology (2014)
  • B.S. - Al Azhar University (2007)

Dr. Sherif's excellence in research has been recognized with the Aubrey Keith and Ella Ginn Lucas Endowment for Faculty Excellence Award (USM, 2022). He has secured multiple internal and external grants to support his research.

Research and Publications

Dr. Sherif's work has been featured in top-tier IEEE conferences and journals, including IEEE ICC, IEEE VTC, IEEE TDSC, IEEE TVT, and IEEE IoT Journal.

He also serves as reviewer and editor for prestigious journals and conferences including IEEE TVT, IEEE IoT Journal, IEEE TDSC, and the Journal of Peer-to-Peer Networking.

Areas of Expertise

  • Cybersecurity and Network Security
  • Machine Learning in Cybersecurity
  • Privacy-Preserving Schemes in Autonomous Vehicles (AVs)
  • Vehicular Ad Hoc Networks (VANETs)
  • Internet of Things (IoT) Applications
  • Smart Grid Advanced Metering Infrastructure (AMI) Networks

Professional Affiliations
